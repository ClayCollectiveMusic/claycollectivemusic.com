Our Story

Clay Collective started with a simple idea: the local church deserves original worship music that sounds like its own voice. The name comes from the picture of jars of clay. We're ordinary people, shaped by a faithful God, and the music that comes out of that is something only real community can carry.

The Beginning

Matt Greiner & Ryan Kurtz Launch Clay Collective

It started with Matt Greiner and Ryan Kurtz. They were friends who loved worship and wanted to write songs that churches could actually sing. Not from a Nashville writing room, but from prayer rooms and weekend gatherings. That early season produced Living Stones, Clay Collective's debut album and the foundation for everything that followed.

A New Chapter

Josh Smucker & Bronley Plumb Pick Up the Mantle

After Living Stones, the vision got passed forward. Josh Smucker and Bronley Plumb stepped in and sharpened the focus, centering Clay Collective specifically on congregational, corporate worship. The goal was clear: write songs that a room full of people can sing together on a Sunday morning. Songs with theological weight that are still simple enough for anyone to follow along.

Growing the Team

Ben Ord Joins the Collective

About a year later, Ben Ord came on board, bringing new musical depth and creative energy. With his addition, the writing and production took a significant step forward. The team was growing, and so was the sound.

The Bigger Picture

A Regional Worship Community

Clay Collective has never been about just one church or one band. Over the years, we've pulled in musicians, vocalists, and worship leaders from churches across the region. We write together, lead together, and record together. Some of our earlier recordings were done at Attic Studio, but more recently we've started recording in house, which gives us the freedom to capture songs in the same rooms where they were first sung.
